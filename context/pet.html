Research-Backed Rationale

Virtual companion mechanics are most useful when they represent meaningful progress inside an already useful product. The strongest product direction for Mentoria is not a standalone mini-game, but a companion that reflects course progress, saved opportunities, deadline readiness, and reflective study habits.

Source Finding Mentoria Implication
StudyGotchi A Tamagotchi-like study avatar in a programming course produced mixed results, highlighting that mechanics and integration quality matter. Do not add a cosmetic pet only. Tie it to real learning events and dashboard utility.
Virtual pet reading platform Reading logs and discussion tasks can be converted into care resources, growth visualization, quests, and analytics. Use lessons, quizzes, saved opportunities, and reflections as the resources that grow the companion.
Gamification meta-analysis Gamification can improve intrinsic motivation modestly, with stronger effects on autonomy and relatedness than competence. Offer choice, supportive feedback, and connection instead of raw point pressure.
Inner Dragon RCT summary A digital pet layer increased app engagement and use of core tools, while final outcome effects remained mixed. Measure both engagement and actual learning actions; do not assume pet usage equals educational impact.
ICO nudge guidance Children should not be nudged into unnecessary data sharing, lowered privacy, excessive engagement, or manipulative choices. Default to high privacy, gentle reminders, pause controls, and no guilt-based mechanics.

MVP Product Concept

MentorPet / Hub Companion is a small persistent companion in the student dashboard and course flow. It encourages students to take useful next steps, celebrates effort, and visualizes progress across Mentoria Hub.

  • During onboarding, the student chooses the companion style, name, and optional nickname. Russian-friendly naming examples: “Менторик”, “Хабик”, “Учёный спутник”.
  • The companion appears in the dashboard as a compact status widget with level, mood, XP progress, and one recommended action.
  • Inside course pages, it gives short supportive feedback after lesson completion, quiz attempts, and progress milestones.
  • In the opportunities catalog, it can suggest saving a relevant opportunity or checking an upcoming deadline.
  • The companion grows through stages such as egg, starter, explorer, scholar, and mentor-in-training.
  • Accessories and visual states unlock through learning milestones, not random rewards or paid mechanics.

MVP Mechanics

Evolution stage Longer-term course and roadmap progress. Advance stage at explicit XP thresholds or milestone counts.
Accessories Trying different product areas such as courses, events, and deadlines. Unlock non-random cosmetic items after meaningful actions.

Pause mode Healthy breaks during exams, vacation, or burnout. Freeze reminders and continuity messaging without penalty.

Ethical Design Rules

The target audience includes teenage students, so the companion must support autonomy and wellbeing. It should motivate without creating emotional debt.

Do Avoid
Use supportive, informational feedback that explains progress. Do not shame users for missed days or unfinished tasks.
Offer pause mode and clear notification controls. Do not make streak repair paid, hidden, or emotionally pressured.
Default to high privacy and collect only necessary companion data. Do not collect sensitive emotional data for personalization in v1.
Let students customize name and appearance in simple ways. Do not use loot boxes, random rewards, or artificial scarcity.
Use companion prompts to point back to learning, deadlines, and reflection. Do not make the companion an open-ended social substitute or mentor replacement.
Non-negotiable: the companion must never die, become sick, decay, cry, or imply that the student harmed it by not using the platform.

Technical Model

For v1, keep companion behavior deterministic. XP, mood, evolution, unlocks, and recommended actions should be derived from explicit product events and server-side rules, not from LLM generation.

Entity Suggested Columns Purpose
companions user_id, name, species, stage, mood, energy, xp, last_interaction_at, pause_until One active companion state per student profile.
companion_events user_id, event_type, source_type, source_id, xp_delta, created_at Append-only audit log for XP and companion state changes.
companion_items id, item_type, name, unlock_rule, asset_key Catalog of non-random cosmetic unlocks.
user_companion_items user_id, item_id, unlocked_at, equipped_at Tracks unlocked and equipped companion items.
companion_messages id, trigger, locale, body, tone, enabled Admin-controlled message templates for safe encouragement.

Use RLS so each student can read and update only their own companion state through approved Server Actions. Admins can manage item definitions, reward rules, and message templates.

Server Actions and Data Flow

  1. Student completes a platform action such as lesson completion, quiz submission, saving an event, or adding a deadline.
  2. The existing domain Server Action completes the main action first.
  3. The Server Action calls a companion service function such as awardCompanionXp(actionType, sourceId).
  4. The service validates the action, inserts a companion_events record, updates companions, and returns the updated companion summary.
  5. The UI renders a small confirmation: XP gained, stage progress, optional unlocked item, and one suggested next step.

Recommended initial action types: lesson_completed, quiz_completed, event_saved, deadline_added, reflection_added, course_enrolled, and roadmap_step_done.

Admin Considerations

  • Admins should be able to enable or disable companion messages by trigger and locale.
  • Admins should tune XP values and stage thresholds without code changes once the MVP stabilizes.
  • Admins should see aggregate analytics such as companion activation rate, XP events by type, and whether companion prompts correlate with course completion.
  • Admins must not see private student reflections unless a separate safety or moderation policy explicitly allows it.

Future AI Extensions

AI is not required for v1. When added, it should remain bounded and safe.

  • Generate short encouragement from approved templates and student-visible context only.
  • Explain why an opportunity or course was recommended using tags, grade level, interests, and deadlines.
  • Offer reflective coaching prompts such as “What helped you finish this lesson?” without diagnosing emotions or mental health.
  • Use moderation and fallback templates for every generated message.

AI Developer Mandates

  • Implement MentorPet / Hub Companion as an optional support layer inside the existing product journey.
  • Keep v1 deterministic and auditable through PostgreSQL rows and Server Actions.
  • Make companion progress reflect meaningful learning and planning actions, not raw login frequency.
  • Include pause mode before adding any streak-like behavior.
  • Use high-privacy defaults and avoid collecting sensitive emotional data.
  • Never use companion distress, loss, death, or guilt as a retention mechanic.
  • Localize companion messages for English, Russian, and Kazakh through admin-controlled templates.
  • Measure educational outcomes separately from companion engagement.
